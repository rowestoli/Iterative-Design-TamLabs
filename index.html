TamLabs.ai – Iterative Design Project

Brown University CS1300

Project Overview

Our team worked on an unguided project for TamLabs.ai, a startup offering an AI Copilot for Microsoft Word. This project followed our course’s iterative design process: (1) Sketching, (2) Wireframing, (3) Critique, and (4) Hi-Fi Prototype & Testing.

We aimed to design a multi-screen, interactive prototype showing how the interface could help users draft and refine documents directly in Word.

About TamLabs

TamLabs focuses on integrating Large Language Models into Microsoft Word as a writing assistant for automation and efficiency. Our design challenge: How can we incorporate AI-driven suggestions and editing into the user’s writing flow with minimal disruption to the familiar Word environment?

Sketching & Wireframing

Each team member explored diverse layouts and functionalities for the TamLabs interface. We combined our top ideas into a low-fidelity wireframe, focusing on three core screens:

Ali's Sketches

Explored a sidebar-based suggestion panel with an inline chat prompt for direct AI interaction.

Andrew's Sketches

Emphasized a simple toggle to enable/disable the Copilot, plus a “rewrite” button for quick AI rewrites of selected text.

Maria's Sketches

Proposed a “document status” overview, showing revision history and AI-generated suggestions in a collapsible timeline.

Rowe's Sketches

Focused on user onboarding: an initial setup screen to gather user preferences (formal vs. casual tone, language style, etc.).
